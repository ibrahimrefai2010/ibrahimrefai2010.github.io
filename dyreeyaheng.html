Diriyah

Diriyah, located on the banks of Wadi Hanifa north of the capital, Riyadh, is considered one of the most important historical cities in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. The city holds great historical significance, as it was the center of rule for the First Saudi State and the birthplace of the religious and political movement established by Imam Muhammad bin Saud in the mid-18th century. Diriyah has become a symbol of Saudi heritage and an important destination for those seeking the Kingdom’s rich history. Diriyah is distinguished by its strategic geographical location between plains and plateaus, which provided it with defensive advantages and supported trade activities. The city quickly flourished to become an important political and religious center, attracting scholars, reformers, and preachers. The At-Turaif district in Diriyah is one of its most prominent historical landmarks. It is the district that housed the palace of Imam Muhammad bin Saud and contains old buildings constructed from mud and stone using traditional architectural styles that reflect the spirit of the local environment and the culture of the region’s people. Diriyah is not merely a historical city but also a symbol of national and cultural identity. The city witnessed many historical events that shaped the path of the modern Saudi state. It also preserves its urban and architectural heritage, as the At-Turaif district has been classified as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in recognition of its historical and artistic value. This classification has helped attract both local and international tourists who wish to explore the Kingdom’s rich past and enjoy the beauty of traditional buildings and old markets that reflect daily life during that era. In addition to its historical importance, Diriyah has transformed in recent years into a cultural and tourist center. The city has witnessed significant efforts to preserve its heritage.
