Old Jeddah

Old Jeddah is considered one of the most ancient historical areas in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. It is a city that combines authenticity and modernity, and within its narrow alleys and clay houses lies the fragrance of a history that dates back many centuries. Jeddah is located on the coast of the Red Sea, and its geographical location played an important role in making it a commercial port and an important cultural center since ancient times. The old Port of Jeddah served as a gateway for pilgrims arriving from different parts of the world to Makkah, which earned the city the "The Eastern Gateway to the Two Holy Mosques." Old Jeddah is distinguished by what is known as “Al-Balad,” the historic district that represents the heart of the old city. Scattered throughout this district are traditional houses built from stone, wood, and plaster, decorated with carved wooden windows locally known as “Rawasheen.” These houses reflect the craftsmanship of ancient artisans in construction and decorative arts, as well as the social and cultural lifestyle of residents in past eras. The narrow alleys of the old town intertwine in a way that resembles a carefully woven network. These alleys served as a refuge for merchants and residents alike, filled with traditional markets and cafés. Souq Al-Alawi, Souq Al-Badu, and Souq Al-Sagha are among the most prominent markets in Old Jeddah. These markets carry a long history of trading goods and traditional products, displaying local handicrafts that reflect authentic Saudi heritage. Within these markets, the past meets the present, as some families continue to preserve their traditional professions across generations, making visitors feel as if they have traveled back in time. Old Jeddah has received significant attention from authorities concerned with heritage preservation. Projects have been established to restore old houses and convert them into museums and cultural centers. The historic district was also registered as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in recognition of its historical and architectural importance. These initiatives reflect the community’s commitment to preserving the city’s cultural identity and enabling future generations to learn about the history and civilization of their ancestors. In addition to its historical character, Old Jeddah is distinguished by the beauty of the Red Sea overlooking its coast, where visitors can combine exploring the alleys of the past with enjoying breathtaking sea views. The old city also contains many religious landmarks, such as historic mosques that tell stories about the religious and social activities of Jeddah’s residents throughout history. In conclusion, Old Jeddah remains a living symbol of history and culture, and an important destination for anyone wishing to experience the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia’s rich past. Visiting it is a journey through time, carrying the fragrance of history and the beauty of traditional architecture, allowing visitors to witness a civilization that has endured for centuries and emphasizing the importance of preserving heritage as part of our national and cultural identity.
